Personal Health Journal — Daniel Bergman
January – June 2024

---

January 6, 2024

New year, same complaints. I've been exhausted for months — not the kind of tired you fix with a good night's sleep. I go to bed at a reasonable hour and wake up feeling like I didn't sleep at all. My wife has been noticing it too. I've started declining plans in the evening because I simply don't have the energy.

I've also been feeling cold all the time. We keep the apartment at normal temperature but I'm often wearing an extra layer while everyone else is comfortable. Weird thing to notice but it's been consistent.

---

January 21, 2024

Weight has been creeping up — I'd estimate I've gained about 6kg over the past year without any real change in what I eat. My clothes fit differently. I've been trying to be more active but nothing seems to shift it.

Constipation has been a recurring issue too — maybe 2-3 times a week I feel backed up. Not severe but uncomfortable and persistent.

---

February 8, 2024

Scheduled an appointment with Dr. Rosen. I've been putting it off but the combination of things — the fatigue, the weight, the cold sensitivity, occasional brain fog — felt like enough to warrant a checkup. She was thorough, asked about sleep, mood, diet, exercise. Ordered a full blood panel covering thyroid, cholesterol, glucose, and a bunch of other things. Results in about a week.

---

February 14, 2024

Brain fog is really getting to me at work. I'm a software engineer and I used to be able to hold complex problems in my head for hours. Lately I keep losing the thread — I'll be mid-thought debugging something and just lose it. Having to write everything down much more than I used to. Colleagues haven't said anything but I notice.

Also noticing my hair feels dry and there's more of it in the shower than I'd like. Probably overthinking it.

---

March 8, 2024

Got the blood test results back from the March 5th test. A lot to process. Dr. Rosen went through everything:

- TSH is 4.4 — technically above the upper limit of 4.0, with T4 at the low end. She called it subclinical/borderline hypothyroidism. Not severe enough for medication yet, but concerning given my symptoms.
- LDL is 152, total cholesterol 218 — both high. Triglycerides also high at 168.
- Fasting glucose at 103 — she said this is technically pre-diabetic territory (100–125) and if it keeps going up, it becomes a real problem.
- Vitamin D at 19 — low, start supplementing.
- CRP at 6.8 — mild inflammation.

She's recommending lifestyle intervention: Mediterranean diet, cut refined carbs and saturated fat, regular exercise. Vitamin D supplement, omega-3 for triglycerides. Retest in 5 months.

I left feeling a bit stunned. I knew something was off but this is a longer list than I expected. Going to take it seriously.

---

March 20, 2024

Started Vitamin D3 (3000 IU per day) and Omega-3 fish oil (2000 mg per day) as Dr. Rosen recommended. Going to start changing my diet this week — less red meat, more fish, more vegetables, cut back on the daily biscuits and sugary snacks.

Still tired but at least I have a direction now.

---

April 10, 2024

Two and a half weeks into the Mediterranean diet approach. I've committed to it — more fish (salmon twice a week, sardines occasionally), olive oil replacing butter, lots of vegetables, lentils and chickpeas replacing some of the pasta and white rice. I'm genuinely enjoying some of the food, especially the salads with olive oil.

Also started the selenium supplement (100 mcg/day) — I read that selenium is involved in thyroid function and that many people with thyroid issues are deficient. Started this on my own after some reading. And magnesium glycinate (300 mg before bed) — read it might help with sleep and the constipation.

Haven't noticed a dramatic difference yet. Still tired. Constipation a bit better. Cold sensitivity unchanged.

---

May 5, 2024

Started a 20-minute morning walk, every weekday before work. I live near a park so it's not a hardship — just a matter of making myself do it. After two weeks I'm at about 14 out of 17 days, which feels like success.

Started taking CoQ10 (200 mg per day) — read about it in the context of heart health and mitochondrial function. Self-initiated, not prescribed.

Energy is still not great but I've had a few mornings this month where I woke up feeling more like myself. Maybe a sign things are starting to shift.

---

June 4, 2024

Two and a half months since starting the supplements and lifestyle changes. Subjective impressions:
- Constipation: noticeably improved. Now maybe once or twice a week rather than 3+ times.
- Cold sensitivity: slightly less constant. Still happening but less extreme.
- Brain fog: some days significantly better, some days still present. Net improvement.
- Energy: slightly improved. The worst days are less bad. Still not where I want to be.
- Weight: down about 2kg from peak. Slow progress but moving in right direction.

Looking forward to the blood test in August to see if the numbers match how I'm feeling.

---

June 25, 2024

Had a good week. Went on a long hike with my family on Saturday — 3 hours — and managed it fine. Four months ago I would have been completely wiped out by that. Still tired by the end but recovered faster than expected.

Whatever combination of the supplements, diet, and walking is doing — it seems to be doing something.
