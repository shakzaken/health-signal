Medication and Supplement Log — Amir Cohen — 2024
==================================================

Started after Type 2 Diabetes diagnosis confirmed by blood test on 15/01/2024 (HbA1c 7.4%).

---

METFORMIN (Glucophage)
Type: Prescription medication
Dose: 500 mg twice daily (initial)
Form: tablet — taken with food to reduce GI side effects
Prescribed by: Dr. Yael Friedman, Internal Medicine
Started: January 20, 2024
Reason: Type 2 Diabetes — HbA1c 7.4% at diagnosis. First-line medication for T2DM.
GI side effects on initiation: nausea, abdominal discomfort, loose stools for approximately 2 weeks.
Resolved by taking tablet mid-meal.

Dose increased to 850 mg twice daily on: March 10, 2024
Reason for increase: HbA1c response required further lowering toward target of < 6.5%.
GI tolerance: acceptable after 3–4 weeks of adjustment.

Important note: Metformin is known to impair Vitamin B12 absorption in the terminal ileum with
long-term use. Patient B12 monitored at every blood test. July 2024 confirmed B12 depletion
(156 pg/mL, LOW). Vitamin B12 supplementation initiated as a result.

Current status: Ongoing as of December 2024 (850 mg twice daily).
Dr. Friedman indicated potential dose reduction discussion at next visit given HbA1c 5.9%.

Type: Supplement — prescribed
Dose: 4000 IU per day
Form: softgel capsule
Prescribed by: Dr. Yael Friedman

OMEGA-3 FISH OIL
Type: Supplement — self-initiated
Dose: 2000 mg per day (EPA/DHA combined)
Form: softgel capsule
Brand: Nordic Naturals Ultimate Omega
Started: February 15, 2024
Reason: Self-initiated after reading about Omega-3 for triglyceride reduction. Triglycerides at
diagnosis were 245 mg/dL (ref < 150). Dr. Friedman was informed and had no objection.
Note: Triglyceride trend — 245 (January) → 178 (July) → 142 (December). Normalized.
Contribution vs. dietary changes uncertain.
Current status: Ongoing as of December 2024

---

VITAMIN B12 (methylcobalamin — sublingual)
Type: Supplement — prescribed
Dose: 1000 mcg per day
Form: sublingual tablet (dissolves under tongue — bypasses GI absorption to avoid Metformin interference)
Prescribed by: Dr. Yael Friedman
Started: July 15, 2024
Reason: July blood test showed B12 at 156 pg/mL (ref 200–900) — LOW. Metformin-induced B12
depletion confirmed. Sublingual formulation specifically chosen to bypass the intestinal
absorption pathway blocked by Metformin.
Note: B12 improved from 156 (July) to 312 (December) after 5 months of supplementation.
Patient reported improvement in peripheral tingling symptoms in feet (possible early neuropathy)
following B12 initiation.
Current status: Ongoing as of December 2024. Must continue as long as Metformin is used.
