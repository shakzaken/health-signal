Personal Health Journal — Daniel Bergman
July – December 2024

---

July 8, 2024

Feeling noticeably better than January. The morning walks have become a real habit — I've extended them to 30 minutes and I actually look forward to them. There's something about having that time outside before the workday starts that sets the tone.

Cold sensitivity: I haven't thought about it much this past month. Wearing the same layers as everyone else again. Hard to say exactly when it shifted.

Brain fog: much improved. I can hold complex work problems in my head again. Finished a difficult debugging session yesterday that took three hours of concentrated effort — that would have been impossible in January.

Energy: consistently better. Still not 25-year-old energy but functional and predictable.

---

July 22, 2024

Added some light strength training — two sessions per week at the gym near work. I've been doing it for three weeks. Nothing intense, just compound movements: squats, rows, press. My goal is not to be a gym person but to build enough muscle to help with metabolism long-term.

Weight is down 4kg from the January peak. Progress is steady and non-miserable.

---

August 12, 2024

Got the August blood test results back. Dr. Rosen seemed genuinely pleased:

- TSH is now 3.1 — back in normal range (was 4.4 in March).
- Fasting glucose normalized to 97 — fully out of pre-diabetic territory.
- Triglycerides down to 142 — just in normal range.
- CRP down to 3.2 — inflammation normalized.
- LDL still elevated (136) but improved from 152. She said keep going.
- Vitamin D still low (28) — not there yet. She increased the recommendation to 4000 IU/day.

Overall she called it an excellent response to lifestyle intervention. The changes are working.

One disappointment: Vitamin D still below 30 even on 3000 IU. Increased my dose to 4000 IU per day starting today.

---

September 10, 2024

Sleep has been good. I get to bed by 10:30pm most nights — this has been a deliberate change from my previous habit of 12am+. The magnesium glycinate before bed might be contributing. Whatever the reason, I wake up refreshed more often than not now. Huge difference from January when I woke up exhausted every day.

A friend mentioned I seemed different — more present in conversation, more energetic. It's nice when other people notice.

---

October 3, 2024

New symptom this month — some lower back stiffness and occasional aches in my knees, especially after sitting for long periods at work. I've been at my desk 8-9 hours a day on a difficult project. This feels more like a sedentary work issue than a health issue, but I'm noting it.

Addressed it by setting a timer to stand and stretch every 90 minutes during work. Helped a lot.

---

October 20, 2024

The work crunch is over. During the intense phase last month I skipped several morning walks and one gym session a week. Noticed my energy dipping and the lower back stiffness getting worse during that period. As soon as I resumed the full routine things improved again. This tells me the lifestyle changes aren't just background noise — they're actively doing something.

Reduced alcohol: used to have 3-4 drinks per week (a glass of wine with dinner most evenings). Cut back to 1-2 per week a few months ago. Not sure if it's contributed to the cholesterol improvement or just general wellbeing but I feel better for it.

---

November 18, 2024

Feeling genuinely well. This is the word I keep coming back to — "well." Not just not-sick, but actually good. The contrast to January is stark: I'm walking 30 minutes every morning, going to the gym twice a week, sleeping properly, eating better, and the supplements seem to have helped the numbers as well as the symptoms.

Constipation: resolved. When I think about it, I realize it's been months since it was an issue.
Cold sensitivity: gone. This one is particularly noticeable.
Brain fog: almost entirely resolved.
Energy: stable and good throughout the day.
Weight: down 5kg from the January peak.

Looking forward to the December blood test.

---

December 14, 2024

Got the December blood test results. Everything normalized:

- TSH: 2.4 — fully normal and stable
- LDL: 119 — finally below 130! Best result all year.
- Fasting glucose: 91 — solidly normal
- Vitamin D: 33 — just into normal range for the first time
- CRP: 1.8 — very low inflammation

Dr. Rosen said I don't need to come back for 12 months unless something changes. She called it a textbook response to lifestyle intervention. She noted in my file that pharmacological intervention for the thyroid or cholesterol had been avoided entirely.

I feel like I got my life back this year. Not through anything dramatic — just consistent small changes over many months.
